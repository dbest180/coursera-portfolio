Popular Trinidad Dishes

From street food favorites to holiday classics

Doubles

The ultimate Trini street food and a national treasure. Two soft, fluffy bara filled with curried channa...

The ultimate Trini street food and a national treasure. Two soft, fluffy bara (fried flatbread) filled with curried channa (chickpeas) seasoned with curry, cumin, and pepper. You customize it with your choice of toppings - tamarind sauce, pepper sauce, cucumber chutney, or shadon beni. Best enjoyed hot from the vendor, any time of day or night. It's cheap, filling, and pure comfort in your hands.

Pelau

This is what Trinis make when we want to feed a crowd or celebrate. It's a one-pot wonder...

This is what Trinis make when we want to feed a crowd or celebrate. It's a one-pot wonder - rice cooked with pigeon peas, chicken (or beef), pumpkin, and coconut milk, all caramelized with burnt sugar for that signature dark color and sweet-savory flavor. The African and Caribbean influences shine through in every bite. Sunday lunch classic, party staple, or just because it's pelau weather. Always served with coleslaw and plantain on the side.

Roti

Not just bread - it's an entire meal experience. The roti (flatbread) is soft and flaky...

Not just bread - it's an entire meal experience. The roti (flatbread) is soft and flaky, wrapped around your choice of curry - chicken, beef, goat, shrimp, or vegetables. The Indian influence runs deep here, brought by our Indo-Trinidadian ancestors and transformed into something uniquely ours. We've got different types too - buss-up-shut (torn up shirt), dhalpuri, paratha. Every Trini has their favorite roti shop, and those debates get serious.

Pastelle

This is Christmas in food form. A labor of love - seasoned meat wrapped in cornmeal dough...

This is Christmas in food form. A labor of love - seasoned meat (pork, chicken, or beef) wrapped in cornmeal dough, tied in banana leaves, and steamed to perfection. The recipe comes from our Spanish and Venezuelan heritage, but like everything in Trinidad, we made it our own. Making pastelles is a family affair during the holidays - everyone has a job, from grinding meat to folding leaves. When you taste one, you're tasting generations of tradition.

Mango Chow

Trini street food at its finest - green mango sliced up and seasoned with salt, pepper, garlic...

Trini street food at its finest - green mango sliced up and seasoned with salt, pepper, garlic, and shadon beni (culantro). Some vendors add cucumber and pommecythere too. It's sour, spicy, crunchy, and addictive. You'll see people eating it everywhere - at the beach, after school, at cricket matches. The perfect balance of flavors that makes your mouth water just thinking about it. Summer in a cup.

Bake and Shark

The pride of Maracas Beach. Fried shark served in a fried flatbread called bake...

The pride of Maracas Beach. Fried shark (or now, sometimes bake and fish) served in a fried flatbread called bake, then loaded with your choice of toppings - lettuce, tomatoes, pineapple, coleslaw, tamarind sauce, garlic sauce, pepper sauce. You build it the way you want it. After a day in the sea, this hits different. It's touristy, yes, but it's also genuinely delicious and a Trini beach tradition you can't skip.
